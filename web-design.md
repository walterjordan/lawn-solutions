### 11. UX Notes for Mobile Conversion

1.  **Sticky CTA is Sacred:** The bottom sticky bar with "Call" and "Get Quote" must always be present once the user scrolls past the initial hero header. It is the primary conversion driver.
2.  **Thumb Zones:** All primary buttons (Quote, Call) must be full width or easily reachable with a right-hand thumb. Minimum tap target size of 44x44 pixels.
3.  **Form Friction:** The quote form must be brutalist in simplicity on mobile. Use appropriate keyboard types (number pad for phone, email keyboard for email). Enable auto-fill.
4.  **Visual Hierarchy:** H1s must be distinct. Body copy needs adequate line height (1.5x) for readability on small screens. Use bullet points freely to break up text.
5.  **Image Loading:** Lazy load all images below the fold. The hero image must be highly compressed (WebP format) for instant Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) scores.
6.  **Click-to-Call:** Every mention of the phone number must be an active `tel:` link.

### 12. Visual Style Guide (Premium Local Aesthetic)

*   **Brand Colors:**
    *   *Primary:* Deep Forest Green (Trust, growth, professional). e.g., #1B4D3E
    *   *Secondary/Accent:* A warm, vibrant color for CTAs to create urgency without looking cheap. A golden-orange or vibrant lime green. e.g., #F7C100 or #7CBD1E.
    *   *Neutral:* Dark Charcoal for text (#333333), Off-white/Light Gray for backgrounds (#F8F9FA) to keep it clean.
*   **Typography:**
    *   *Headlines:* A strong, modern sans-serif font (e.g., Montserrat, Poppins, or Inter) with heavy weights (700/800).
    *   *Body:* A highly readable sans-serif (e.g., Open Sans, Roboto) at 16px base size on mobile.
*   **Imagery:**
    *   **Authentic Only:** No smiling stock photos of models posing with rakes. Use high-quality photos of real local lawns, close-ups of healthy grass texture, and actual photos of crew members in branded uniforms and trucks. The aesthetic should feel sunny, vibrant, and clean.
